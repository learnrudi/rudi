## Critical path (must complete before pitching Hub leads)

### 1. Register RUDI as a SAM.gov entity (separate from personal registration)
**Time:** 2–4 weeks end-to-end. Start this week.

Brandon already has a personal SAM.gov registration under `brandonzhoff@gmail.com`. That's not sufficient — NSF subawards go to entities, not individuals. Steps:

1. Log into SAM.gov with existing credentials.
2. Start a **new entity registration** for the RUDI legal entity (LLC or whatever the filed name is).
3. You'll need:
   - Legal entity name + DBA (if applicable)
   - EIN (from IRS — if you don't have one for RUDI yet, apply at irs.gov/ein)
   - Physical address (PO Box not allowed for UEI assignment)
   - Banking info for direct deposit
   - Primary NAICS codes: 611430, 611710, 541611, 541990
4. Submit — SAM will assign a **UEI (Unique Entity Identifier)**.
5. Federal activation takes 7–14 business days after submission.

**Done when:** RUDI has its own UEI and shows as "Active" in SAM.gov, distinct from Brandon's personal registration.

### 2. Confirm the RUDI legal entity

### 3. Insurance — E&O + Cyber
**Time:** 1–2 weeks for quotes + underwriting.

Most university subawards require:
- **General Liability:** $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate
- **Professional Liability (E&O):** $1M per claim / $1M aggregate
- **Cyber Liability:** $1M aggregate (required when handling any participant data — and you will)

Typical annual cost for a firm at RUDI's stage: $2,500–$5,000/yr combined.

Request quotes from:
- Hiscox (direct, online, fast for small professional services firms)
- The Hartford (direct, broader coverage)
- An independent broker if you want competitive quotes across carriers

**Done when:** certificates of insurance (COIs) on file, with lead universities named as additional insureds when you have named partners.
