Outcomes

What participants build

Camp Claude is not a passive tutorial. The goal is to turn Claude into a structured work partner with repeatable habits and visible outputs.

Workflow

Personal workflow map

A short map of where Claude fits into the participant's real work, including where human review stays required.

Prompt System

Reusable prompt kit

Prompts for research, document review, drafting, revision, summarization, meeting prep, and next-step planning.

Quality

Verification checklist

A simple review pattern for catching hallucinations, weak assumptions, privacy risk, missing sources, and overconfident outputs.

Cohort Path

Five labs, one usable workflow

The cohort path can run as a short public workshop, a private team training, or a deeper RUDI Workflow Camp engagement.

Lab 01

Claude as work partner

Set expectations, use cases, risk boundaries, and the difference between chat and workflow.

Lab 02

Prompt systems

Build reusable instructions, role context, constraints, examples, and review criteria.

Lab 03

Document workflows

Use Claude for review, synthesis, summarization, redlines, checklists, and decision memos.

Lab 04

Research and planning

Turn messy information into briefs, agendas, outreach plans, project plans, and follow-ups.

Lab 05

Team handoff

Package the workflow, define quality gates, and decide what moves into team practice.

Audience

Who should use it

Camp Claude is strongest when participants already have real work to improve. It is designed for practical adoption, not generic AI awareness.

Business

Operators and teams

People responsible for sales, client delivery, operations, research, documentation, and recurring knowledge work.

Education

Educators and workforce leaders

Teams building AI fluency for staff, learners, or adult professionals who need applied practice.

Executive

Leaders testing AI adoption

Decision makers who need to understand what safe, useful AI workflows look like before scaling tools across a team.
